\begin{abstract}
Methods for quantifying the effects of uncertainties in hyperbolic problems can be divided into intrusive and non-intrusive techniques. A main drawback of intrusive methods is the need to implement new code, whereas collocation methods recycle a given deterministic solver. Unlike intrusive methods, non-intrusive methods yield a decoupled system of equations, meaning that they are embarrassingly parallel. Moreover, the coupled system of the intrusive stochastic-Galerkin (SG) method is not necessarily hyperbolic. The Intrusive Polynomial Moment (IPM) method fixes this issue at the cost of having to solve an optimization problem in every spatial cell and every time step.
In this work, we discuss advantages of intrusive methods and propose acceleration techniques for SG and IPM. When solving steady problems, the numerical costs arising from repeatedly solving the IPM optimization problem can be reduced by borrowing ideas from shape optimization. By integrating the iteration to solve the optimization problem into the moment update, we can guarantee local convergence while reducing numerical costs. Furthermore, we propose to use non-intrusive methods as a preconditioner for intrusive methods. Consequently, a large amount of iterations to the steady solution are performed by a cheap method, while maintaining the increased accuracy of an intrusive method. Additionally, we propose an adaptive implementation of the IPM method, which further reduces numerical costs. We demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed strategies by comparing results of the uncertain NACA0012 testcase as well as a bent shock tube experiment.
\end{abstract}
